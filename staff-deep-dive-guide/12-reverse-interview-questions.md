# Reverse Interview — Conceptual Depth (What to Ask & Why)

---

## 1. Purpose of “your questions” round

**Beginner view:** Polite closing.  
**Reality:** You **signal** **curiosity**, **judgment**, and **cultural** **fit**; you **de-risk** your own **accept** decision.

**Staff angle:** Questions should **not** be **generic HR filler**—they should **surface** **constraints** that change how you’d **operate** (SLO culture, **debt**, **politics**, **tech** **strategy**).

---

## 2. How to structure a strong question

**Formula:** **Context** + **specific** **question** + **optional** “I’m asking because …”

**Example (weak):** “Do you like microservices?”  
**Example (strong):** “In my last role **API** **versioning** **disputes** burned **weeks**—how do **internal** teams here **govern** **compatibility** and **deprecation** for **partner-facing** **trafficking** **APIs**?”

**Follow-up power:** When they answer, **reflect** one phrase back: “You said **reconciliation** is **painful**—is that **mostly** **data** **quality** or **organizational** **ownership**?”

---

## 3. Question bank with **intent** (what you learn)

### Org risk

**Q:** “What are the **top 2–3** **technical** risks **this year** for **this** org?”  
**Intent:** Do they **think** **portfolio-level**? Is answer **vague** or **specific** (names systems)?

### Staff success

**Q:** “What does **strong** **performance** look like at **90** vs **365** days for this **Staff** slot?”  
**Intent:** **Clarity** on **multiplier** expectations vs **IC** **heroics**.

### Cross-functional reality

**Q:** “Walk me through how a **typical** **revenue-affecting** **incident** flows—**who** **pages**, **who** **decides** **mitigation**, **who** **talks** to **Ad** **Ops**?”  
**Intent:** **On-call** **maturity** and **respect** for **operators**.

### Modernization

**Q:** “What’s the **largest** **legacy** **dependency** **left** in **ad** / **scheduling**—and what **incremental** **plan** exists, if any?”  
**Intent:** Are they **honest** about **debt**? **Blame** or **ownership** tone?

### Data & truth

**Q:** “Where do **finance**-grade **numbers** come from **today**—and how do **real-time** **dashboards** **relate**?”  
**Intent:** **Lambda** / **reconciliation** maturity—highly relevant to **your** **design** **answers**.

### SLO culture

**Q:** “What **SLOs** exist for **decision** **latency** / **availability**—and has an **error** **budget** **ever** **stopped** a **launch**?”  
**Intent:** Is **SLO** **real** or **theater**?

### Conflict resolution

**Q:** “When **Product** wants a **shortcut** that **hurts** **reliability**, how is that **resolved** **in** **practice**?”  
**Intent:** **Exec** **alignment** vs **endless** **fight**.

### Growth

**Q:** “How do **Staff** ICs **mentor** here—**formal** **programs** or **ad** **hoc**?”  
**Intent:** **Investment** in **senior** **talent**.

---

## 4. Questions that often flop (and alternatives)

| Weak | Stronger |
|------|----------|
| “How’s work-life balance?” | “How is **on-call** **load** **measured** and **compensated**?” |
| “Do you have **tech** **debt**?” | “What **debt** **block** **H1** **roadmap**—**who** **owns** **paydown**?” |
| “**Excited** about **AI**?” | “Where is **ML** **actually** in **production** **today** for **yield** / **forecasting**?” |

---

## 5. Closing moves

- “Based on what I heard about **X**, I’d **focus** **first** **90** days on **Y**—does that **align** with **your** **hope**?”  
- “Is there **anything** **about** **my** **background** that **concerns** **you**—I’d **love** to **address** it.” (**Brave** but **useful**.)

---

## 6. Worked examples (mini dialogues)

### Dialogue A — After system design round

**You:** “You mentioned **reconciliation** between **real-time** and **batch** is still **manual** in some markets—**who** **owns** the **runbook** today, **Ad Ops** or **Eng**?”

**They:** “Mostly **Ops** files tickets.”

**You (follow-up):** “Is the **goal** to **automate** **delta** detection this year, or **tolerate** for **longer**? That changes whether I’d **invest** in **streaming** **accuracy** vs **tooling** around **batch** **only**.”

### Dialogue B — Hiring manager

**You:** “What’s **one** **concrete** thing the **last Staff hire** did in **first 6 months** that **you’d** call **exemplary**?”

**Intent:** Reveals **real** expectations—not **JD** fluff.

### Dialogue C — Principal / OE

**You:** “Tell me about a **recent** **near-miss**—system didn’t go **down**, but **risk** was **high**. What **changed** afterward?”

**Intent:** Psychological safety + **learning** culture signal.

### Dialogue D — They ask “Any challenges for you?”

**You:** “I’m **strong** on **data** **pipelines**; I’d **want** a **buddy** for **linear traffic** **nuance** **first** quarter—**is there** **budget** for **domain** **pairing**?”

### Dialogue E — Red flag answer (how you note mentally)

**They:** “We **don’t** **really** **believe** in **postmortems**; **just** fix and move.”

**You:** Neutral: “Thanks—that helps me understand **ops** **culture**.” **Internally** you weigh **risk**.
